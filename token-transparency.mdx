# Token Transparency Framework

<Info>
A standardized, open-source disclosure framework for token issuers. Leveling the playing field for all investors.
</Info>

## Overview

The Token Transparency Framework provides token projects with the ability to communicate essential information to the market through a structured, one-time filing focusing on 18 criteria across four categories.

## Framework Structure

### Scoring Methodology
- **Maximum Score**: 40 points
- **Criteria**: 18 weighted disclosure items
- **Weighting**: 0-3 points per criterion based on materiality
- **Categories**: 4 main disclosure categories

## Transparency Scores

Projects are evaluated and receive a transparency score out of 40, representing their commitment to disclosure and openness.

### Score Interpretation
- **35 to 40**: Excellent transparency (87.5% to 100%)
- **30 to 34**: Good transparency (75% to 85%)
- **20 to 29**: Moderate transparency (50% to 72.5%)
- **Below 20**: Poor transparency (less than 50%)

## 18 Disclosure Criteria

The framework evaluates projects across 18 specific criteria, each weighted by materiality (0-3 points):

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="High Materiality (3 points each)">
    1. **Token Allocation & Distribution** - Complete breakdown of token allocation
    2. **Vesting Schedules** - Detailed vesting terms for all stakeholders
    3. **Team & Advisor Holdings** - Full disclosure of insider positions
    4. **Revenue Model** - Clear explanation of how the project generates value
    5. **Treasury Management** - Transparency on fund management
    6. **Market Making Agreements** - Disclosure of any market making arrangements
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Medium Materiality (2 points each)">
    7. **Project Purpose** - Clear mission and objectives
    8. **Financial Statements** - Regular financial reporting
    9. **Related Party Transactions** - Disclosure of insider dealings
    10. **Equity-Token Relationship** - Clarity on token vs equity rights
    11. **Lock-up Provisions** - Token lock-up terms and conditions
    12. **Governance Structure** - Decision-making framework
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Standard Materiality (1-2 points each)">
    13. **Team Verification** - KYC and background of team members
    14. **Funding History** - Past fundraising rounds and valuations
    15. **Technology Audits** - Security audit reports
    16. **Legal Structure** - Corporate entity information
    17. **Regulatory Compliance** - Adherence to applicable regulations
    18. **Risk Disclosures** - Key risk factors and disclaimers
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Implementation Timeline

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initial Submission">
    Projects complete the comprehensive disclosure form covering all 18 criteria
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Process">
    Independent review and verification of submitted information
  </Step>

  <Step title="Scoring">
    Assignment of transparency score based on completeness and materiality
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publication">
    Public release of transparency report and score
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ongoing Updates">
    Annual updates or material change disclosures as needed
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to Participate

Projects interested in demonstrating their commitment to transparency can:

1. **Review the Framework** - Understand the 18 disclosure criteria
2. **Prepare Documentation** - Gather required information and documents
3. **Submit Application** - Complete the standardized disclosure form
4. **Undergo Review** - Participate in the verification process
5. **Receive Score** - Get your transparency rating and report

<Note>
The transparency score is not a quality assessment of the team or project. Rather, it measures the project's overall commitment to transparency and disclosure.
</Note>
